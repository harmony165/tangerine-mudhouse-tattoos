About

the hand & the lineage

Mourya is a Bengaluru-based tattoo artist whose work is rooted in the living tradition of Pattachitra — the ancient scroll-painting art of Odisha. Each tattoo is a collaboration between the wearer's story and centuries of sacred visual language: deities, animals, and geometric cosmologies rendered in fine ink.

FAQ

Send a message on Instagram DM (@asura66.6) or WhatsApp (+91 70086 70841). Share your idea, placement on the body, and any reference images — Mourya will get back to you to discuss details and schedule a session.

Slots typically fill 2–4 weeks ahead. For larger or more complex pieces — backpieces, sleeves, full-day sessions — reaching out several weeks in advance is strongly recommended.

Eat a proper meal before arriving, stay well-hydrated, and get a good night's sleep. Wear or bring clothing that gives easy access to the area being tattooed. Avoid alcohol for at least 24 hours before your appointment.

Pain varies by placement and individual tolerance. Bony or sensitive areas (ribs, spine, inner arm, ankles) tend to be more intense; fleshy areas are generally more manageable. Most people find the experience very tolerable once they settle in.

The outer skin typically heals within 2–3 weeks. Full healing through the deeper skin layers can take 3–6 months. Following aftercare instructions carefully is essential to preserving the quality of the tattoo long-term.

Keep the tattoo clean and lightly moisturised, avoid soaking in water, direct sun exposure, and picking at any peeling skin. Detailed aftercare instructions will be provided at the end of your session.

Mourya works across three disciplines: Traditional Electric Tattooing, Handpoke, and Paintings — each carrying its own quality of mark and feeling on skin or surface.

His foremost speciality is Pattachitra-inspired sacred work — deities, cosmic geometries, and natural motifs drawn from the ancient scroll-painting tradition of Odisha. He also practises Traditional Japanese Tattooing (Irezumi), the centuries-old art of bold, iconographic imagery — koi, dragons, oni, peony, and wave — executed with the weight and intention that earned Irezumi its place as one of the world's great tattoo traditions. Alongside these, Mourya takes on detailed blackwork, fine-line, and figurative compositions.
